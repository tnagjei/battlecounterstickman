My Winter Car Build

Learn how to build your car in My Winter Car step by step.

Build Process

  • Start with chassis and suspension
  • Install engine and transmission
  • Connect fuel and electrical systems
  • Add body panels and interior

Getting Started

Building the car in My Winter Car is a detailed process that requires patience and organization. Before you begin, gather the parts catalog and familiarize yourself with the basic tools available in your garage. The build process typically takes many in-game days to complete.

Essential Tools

  • Wrench Set: For bolts and nuts
  • Socket Set: For engine components
  • Screwdriver: For electrical and interior
  • Jack: For wheel and suspension work

Tips for Success

Keep your workshop warm while building. Work in short sessions to manage fatigue. Double-check all connections before attempting to start the car. Missing a single bolt can prevent the car from running.

Detailed Build Phases

The complete build process is divided into several distinct phases. Each phase must be completed before moving to the next. Understanding these phases helps you plan your work sessions and order parts in advance.

Phase 1: Chassis and Suspension

Begin by inspecting the chassis in the barn. Install the front suspension struts and rear axle. Mount the steering column and connect the brake lines. This phase establishes the foundation for everything else. Take your time to ensure all bolts are properly tightened.

Phase 2: Engine Assembly

The engine is the most complex component. Start with the engine block and add internal components like pistons and crankshaft. Install the cylinder head and valve train. Connect the starter motor and alternator. This phase requires the most precision and patience.

Phase 3: Drivetrain

Install the transmission and clutch assembly. Connect the driveshaft to the rear axle. Mount the gear shifter inside the cabin. Test the linkage to ensure smooth gear changes before proceeding to the electrical system.

Phase 4: Electrical and Fuel

Wire the ignition system and connect the battery. Install the fuel tank and run fuel lines to the carburetor. Add the radiator and coolant hoses. Test electrical connections before attempting the first start.

Phase 5: Body and Interior

Mount the body panels including doors, hood, and trunk. Install the seats and dashboard. Add windows and mirrors. Complete the interior trim and connect any remaining accessories.

Common Build Problems

  • Car won't start: Check battery charge, fuel supply, and spark plugs
  • No power: Verify all electrical connections and fuses
  • Overheating: Ensure coolant hoses are connected and radiator is full
  • Transmission issues: Check clutch installation and gear linkage

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to build the car?

A: A complete build takes 5-10 in-game days depending on your familiarity with the process and how organized you are with parts.

Q: What should I build first?

A: Start with the suspension and chassis, then move to the engine. Complete electrical work last after all mechanical systems are in place.

Q: Can I save during the build?

A: Yes, save frequently to avoid losing progress. Use the bed in your house to save the game.

Q: What if I'm missing a part?

A: Order from the parts catalog via phone or check the junkyard for used parts. Plan ahead to avoid waiting.
