My Winter Car Beginner Guide

New to My Winter Car? This guide covers survival basics, earning money, and starting your car build.

First Day Priorities

  • Explore your house: Find food, matches, warm clothing
  • Check the sauna: Primary way to stay warm
  • Locate the barn: Your project car awaits
  • Find the phone: For ordering parts and jobs

Survival Basics

Monitor body temperature, hunger, fatigue, and thirst. Use the sauna to warm up, eat regularly, and sleep when tired.

Earning Money

  • Firewood Delivery: Cut and deliver wood
  • Taxi Service: Once you have a vehicle
  • Odd Jobs: Check bulletin boards

Starting Your Car Build

Study the parts in the barn, order the parts catalog, and start with chassis and suspension. Don't rush the process.

Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Ignoring temperature: Always check body temp before going outside
  • Not saving: Save frequently using your bed
  • Rushing the build: Take time to learn each system
  • Getting lost: Learn the map before long trips

Essential Items

Keep these items with you at all times: warm clothing, food supply, flashlight for night, and matches for emergencies. Store extras at home for backup.

Complete Beginner Walkthrough

Starting your journey in My Winter Car can feel overwhelming with so many systems to manage. This comprehensive guide walks you through everything a new player needs to know, from basic survival to completing your first car build.

Understanding the Game Objectives

Your main goal is to rebuild the Corris Rivett car in the barn while surviving the harsh Finnish winter. This requires balancing car building work with survival needs like food, warmth, and rest. Secondary objectives include earning money through various jobs and exploring the open world.

Managing Your Stats

Four primary stats determine your survival. Body temperature drops when exposed to cold, requiring warm clothing and heated spaces. Hunger decreases over time and is restored by eating food. Fatigue builds up through activity and requires sleep to recover. Stress can affect your performance and is managed through various activities.

Your First Week Strategy

Spend the first few days learning the controls and exploring your property. Heat the sauna daily to maintain body temperature. Locate food supplies in your house and the nearby store. Find the phone to start receiving job offers. Don't rush into car building until you understand survival mechanics.

Making Money Early

Firewood delivery is the easiest early money source. Chop wood at the log pile and deliver it to customers. As you progress, taxi jobs become available once you have a running vehicle. Check bulletin boards and the phone for additional job opportunities.

Pro Tips for New Players

  • Save often: Use the bed to save your progress regularly
  • Watch the weather: Avoid outdoor work during storms
  • Stay organized: Keep parts sorted for efficient building
  • Read tutorials: Use the wiki for detailed guides
  • Be patient: The game rewards careful planning

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What should I do on my first day?

A: Explore your house, heat the sauna, find food, and learn the basic controls.

Q: How do I save my progress?

A: Go to your bed and sleep. This saves your game and restores fatigue.

Q: How do I earn money early?

A: Chop firewood and deliver it. Check the phone for job opportunities.
