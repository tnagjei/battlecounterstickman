My Winter Car Best Jobs

Compare all jobs in My Winter Car to find the best way to earn money for car parts.

Job Comparison

  • Taxi: Best pay, requires working car
  • Firewood: Good income, no car needed
  • Mailer: Steady work, moderate pay
  • Log Machine: High pay, requires skill

Recommended Strategy

Start with firewood until you build your car, then switch to taxi for higher earnings.

Complete Job Analysis

Choosing the best job in My Winter Car depends on your current progress and available resources. Each job has distinct advantages and disadvantages that affect your overall income and game progression.

Early Game: Firewood Delivery

For players just starting out, firewood delivery is unquestionably the best choice. You don't need a vehicle, and the tools are readily available. Head to the woodpile near your house, chop logs, and deliver them to customers who call in orders. The pay is moderate but steady, and you can work in any weather. This job allows you to earn money while learning the game mechanics.

Mid Game: Newspaper/Mail Delivery

Once you have basic transportation like a bicycle or sled, newspaper delivery becomes viable. The routes are predictable, and the work is less physically demanding than chopping wood. You can complete routes quickly in the morning and have the rest of the day for car building. This job provides consistent income without requiring a completed car.

Late Game: Taxi Service

With a running car, taxi becomes the undisputed best job for earnings. NPCs around town need rides to various destinations, and longer trips pay significantly more. However, factor in fuel costs and vehicle maintenance. A well-maintained car means fewer breakdowns and more fares completed. This is where your car-building investment pays off.

Detailed Earnings Breakdown

  • Taxi Service: 50-200 marks per fare, 500+ marks daily potential
  • Firewood Delivery: 20-50 marks per load, 150-300 marks daily
  • Newspaper Route: 10-30 marks per route, 100-200 marks daily
  • Log Machine Work: 30-80 marks per session, requires skill

Maximizing Your Income

  • Combine jobs on single trips to maximize time efficiency
  • Work during good weather for outdoor jobs
  • Maintain your vehicle to avoid repair costs eating into profits
  • Learn optimal routes for regular deliveries
  • Build reputation with repeat customers for better opportunities

FAQ

Q: What's the absolute best job for money?

A: Taxi service offers the highest earnings potential, but requires a fully functional car.

Q: Can I do multiple jobs?

A: Yes! Combine jobs like delivering newspapers while picking up taxi fares for maximum efficiency.

Q: How fast can I earn money for parts?

A: With consistent work, expect 200-500 marks daily starting out, increasing significantly with taxi work.
