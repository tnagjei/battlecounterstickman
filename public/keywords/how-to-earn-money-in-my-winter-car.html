Complete Money Making Guide

Money is essential in My Winter Car for buying parts, fuel, food, and supplies. Building the Corris Rivett requires significant investment, so understanding how to earn efficiently is critical to your success.

Before You Have a Car

When starting out without transportation, firewood delivery is your primary income source. Find the axe near your house, chop wood at the log pile, and deliver to customers who call. You can transport wood using a sled or wheelbarrow. This method is labor-intensive but requires no startup investment.

After Building Your Car

Once your Corris Rivett is running, taxi service becomes available. This is the highest-paying job in the game. Pick up passengers and drive them to their destinations for fares that range from 30 marks for short trips to over 200 marks for long-distance rides. The more reliable your car, the more fares you can complete.

Earnings by Job Type

  • Taxi: 300-600 marks daily (best earnings)
  • Firewood: 100-200 marks daily (starting out)
  • Mail/Newspaper: 80-150 marks daily (consistent)
  • Log Machine: 150-300 marks daily (requires skill)

Money Saving Tips

  • Buy used parts from the junkyard when possible
  • Keep your car well-maintained to avoid expensive repairs
  • Cook your own food instead of buying prepared meals
  • Plan trips efficiently to save fuel
  • Stock up on supplies during town visits

Budgeting for Parts

Essential car parts can cost hundreds or thousands of marks. Prioritize engine and suspension components first, as these are required for the car to run. Body panels and cosmetic parts can wait until you have stable income from taxi work.

FAQ

Q: What's the fastest way to earn money?

A: Taxi driving with a completed car offers the best earning rate per hour.

Q: How much money do I need to complete the car?

A: Expect to spend 5,000-10,000 marks total on parts if buying new from the catalog.

Q: Can I earn money without working?

A: Some money can be found around the map, but jobs are required for significant income.
