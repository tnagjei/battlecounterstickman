My Winter Car Parts Catalog

Complete guide to the parts catalog for ordering Corris Rivett components in My Winter Car.

Using the Catalog

  • Find the catalog in your mailbox
  • Browse parts by category
  • Note part numbers and prices
  • Call to place orders

Categories

Engine, drivetrain, suspension, brakes, body, electrical, interior parts available.

Using the Parts Catalog

The parts catalog is your primary source for ordering new car parts. You'll find it in your mailbox at the start of the game. Browse through the pages to find everything from engine components to body panels.

How to Order

  • Open the catalog and find the part you need
  • Note the part number and price
  • Use the phone to call the parts dealer
  • Dial the number and place your order
  • Wait for delivery to your mailbox (1-3 days)

Delivery Times

Small parts typically arrive within one game day. Larger components like body panels may take 2-3 days. Plan ahead and order parts before you need them to avoid waiting.

Complete Catalog Guide

The parts catalog is an essential tool for completing your car build. It contains every part available for order, organized by category. Understanding how to use the catalog efficiently saves time and money throughout your project.

Catalog Organization

The catalog is divided into sections by system. Engine parts are grouped together, followed by transmission, suspension, body, interior, and electrical sections. Each part has a unique number, description, and price listed clearly. Familiarize yourself with the layout to find parts quickly.

Ordering Process Step by Step

  1. Find the part you need in the catalog and note the part number
  2. Write down the price to ensure you have enough money saved
  3. Go to the phone and dial the parts dealer number listed in the catalog
  4. Follow the voice prompts to enter the part number using the keypad
  5. Confirm your order and hang up to complete the transaction

Delivery Schedule Details

Small parts like bolts, gaskets, and electrical components typically arrive within one game day. Medium parts like carburetors, brake components, and interior pieces take two days. Large items such as engine blocks, transmissions, and body panels may require three days for delivery.

Cost Management Tips

  • Budget carefully: Add up costs before ordering large batches of parts
  • Use the junkyard: Save money on non-critical components
  • Order in advance: Avoid waiting during critical assembly phases
  • Keep a list: Track what you've ordered and received
  • Prioritize: Order essential parts first before cosmetic items

Payment and Delivery

Payment is collected when the parts are delivered to your mailbox. Make sure you have sufficient funds available when the delivery arrives. If you can't pay, the parts will be returned and you'll need to reorder them later.

FAQ

Q: Where is the parts catalog?

A: Check your mailbox at the start of the game. It's delivered automatically.

Q: How do I pay for parts?

A: Payment is collected on delivery. Make sure you have enough money before ordering.
