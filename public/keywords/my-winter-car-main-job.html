My Winter Car Main Job

Guide to the main job in My Winter Car.

Main Job

The main job offers the best pay in the game. Complete tasks for reliable income to fund your car project.

Complete Main Job Guide

The main job in My Winter Car provides stable, guaranteed income that can fund your car-building project. Unlike freelance work like firewood or taxi driving, the main job has set hours and consistent pay.

Getting the Job

To get the main job, check the employment listings at the job center or look for job postings in town. Call the listed number to apply. Once accepted, you'll receive a schedule of work hours and a location to report to. Make sure you can reliably get to work on time.

Work Schedule

The main job typically requires you to work set hours, usually morning shifts or full days. You must arrive on time and stay for the duration of your shift. Missing work or arriving late will affect your standing and potentially lead to termination.

Types of Work

  • Factory Work: Assembly line tasks with steady pay
  • Warehouse: Loading and unloading goods
  • Forestry: Tree cutting and processing
  • Construction: Building and repair tasks

Earnings and Benefits

Main job earnings vary by position but typically offer 200-400 marks per day of work. The guaranteed income makes budgeting easier, though the time commitment limits your availability for car building and other activities.

Balancing Work and Car Building

  • Work during mornings, build car in afternoons
  • Use weekends for intensive building sessions
  • Order parts in advance so they arrive on days off
  • Keep survival needs managed to maximize productivity
  • Save money during work periods for parts purchases

FAQ

Q: How do I find the main job?

A: Check bulgin boards around town or call employment numbers in your phone book.

Q: How much does the main job pay?

A: Expect 200-400 marks per day depending on the position and hours worked.

Q: Can I quit the main job?

A: Yes, you can stop working at any time. Consider having taxi income ready as a backup.
