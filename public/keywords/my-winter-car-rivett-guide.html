My Winter Car Rivett Guide - Complete Build Tutorial

The Corris Rivett is the main vehicle you'll rebuild in My Winter Car. This comprehensive guide covers everything from finding your first parts to completing the full assembly and getting the car running.

Understanding the Rivett Build

Building the Corris Rivett is the central challenge of My Winter Car. The car begins as a stripped-down shell in the barn, requiring you to source over 200 individual parts and assemble them in the correct order. This guide will walk you through each step of the process.

Finding Parts for Your Rivett

Parts for the Rivett can be obtained through several methods:

  • Parts Catalog: Order new parts from the magazine that arrives in your mailbox
  • Junkyard: Find used parts at lower prices but varying conditions
  • Around the Map: Some parts are scattered throughout the game world
  • Phone Orders: Call Rivett's number to order specific components

Recommended Assembly Order

While you have flexibility in how you approach the build, following this general order will help avoid frustration:

  • 1. Subframe & Suspension: Install the front and rear subframes first
  • 2. Engine Bay: Mount the engine, transmission, and related components
  • 3. Fuel System: Install fuel tank, lines, and carburetor
  • 4. Electrical: Wire up the battery, starter, and ignition system
  • 5. Interior: Add seats, dashboard, and steering components
  • 6. Body Panels: Finish with fenders, doors, and trim pieces

Common Build Mistakes to Avoid

Many players struggle with these issues when building the Rivett:

  • Forgetting to tighten all bolts - use the wrench on every connection
  • Installing the radiator before the engine - this blocks access
  • Mixing up fuel and brake line connections
  • Not checking oil levels before starting the engine

Expert Tips for Success

These tips will make your Rivett build go much smoother:

  • Keep your garage warm - cold hands work slower
  • Organize parts on shelves before installation
  • Save frequently, especially before major assembly steps
  • Use the inspection feature to check your work
