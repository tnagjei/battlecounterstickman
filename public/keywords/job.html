My Winter Car Jobs - Complete Money Making Guide 2025

Making money is essential for survival in My Winter Car. Various jobs are available throughout the game world, each offering different pay and requirements.

Available Jobs

  • Firewood Delivery: Chop and deliver firewood
  • Taxi Service: Transport NPCs around town
  • Newspaper Delivery: Deliver newspapers to mailboxes
  • Main Job: Regular employment for steady income

Money Making Tips

  • Start with firewood delivery for quick early income
  • Taxi service becomes profitable once you have a working car
  • Balance job time with survival needs
  • Manage fuel costs when calculating profits

Job Locations

Check the phone book for job contacts and locations. Some jobs require specific items or a working vehicle to complete.

Detailed Job Breakdown

Understanding each job's requirements and earnings helps you maximize your income while managing your time effectively. Here's a comprehensive overview of every job available in My Winter Car.

Firewood Delivery

Firewood delivery is the most accessible job for new players. You can start immediately without any vehicle. Head to the woodpile near your house and use the axe to chop logs into firewood. Once you have enough, call customers from the phone to arrange deliveries. Each delivery pays based on the amount of wood delivered. This job is physically demanding but reliable for early income.

Taxi Service

Once you have a working car, taxi service becomes your most profitable option. NPCs around town need transportation to various locations. Pick them up and drive them to their destination to earn money. Longer trips pay more, but factor in fuel costs. Keep your car well-maintained to avoid breakdowns during fares. This job offers the best earnings per hour once established.

Newspaper/Mail Delivery

Newspaper delivery provides steady income with predictable routes. Pick up newspapers from the distribution point and deliver them to mailboxes around the map. This job works well in the early morning and can be combined with other activities. The pay is moderate but consistent, making it good supplementary income.

Main Employment

Regular employment offers guaranteed wages but requires commitment to specific hours. Check the job listings at the employment office or call the listed numbers in your phone book. These jobs provide stable income but limit your flexibility for car building and other activities.

Earnings Comparison

  • Taxi: 50-200 marks per fare (highest potential)
  • Firewood: 20-50 marks per delivery (no startup cost)
  • Newspaper: 10-30 marks per route (consistent)
  • Main Job: Fixed hourly wage (guaranteed income)

Pro Tips for Maximum Earnings

  • Combine multiple jobs in one trip to save time and fuel
  • Check weather before starting outdoor jobs like firewood
  • Keep your car fueled and maintained to avoid lost income
  • Build relationships with regular customers for better opportunities
  • Balance work time with car building progress

FAQ

Q: What's the best job for beginners?

A: Firewood delivery is ideal for beginners since it requires no vehicle and can be started immediately.

Q: How do I find job contacts?

A: Check the phone book in your house for all job-related phone numbers and contact information.

Q: Which job pays the most?

A: Taxi service offers the highest earnings per hour, but requires a working and reliable car.
