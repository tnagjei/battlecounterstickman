My Winter Car Rivett Build - Complete Assembly Guide

Building the Corris Rivett from scratch is the ultimate goal in My Winter Car. This guide covers the complete assembly process from bare chassis to road-ready vehicle.

Before You Start Building

The Rivett build requires preparation. Before diving into assembly, make sure you have:

  • Adequate funds: Building costs around 5000-8000 marks for new parts
  • Warm workspace: Keep the garage heated for comfortable work
  • Tool access: Locate your wrenches and screwdrivers
  • Parts catalog: Check your mailbox for the ordering magazine

Step 1: Chassis and Subframe

Begin with the foundation of your Rivett. The chassis components provide the base for everything else:

  • Mount the front subframe to the chassis
  • Attach suspension components - struts and springs
  • Install the rear axle assembly
  • Secure all bolts with the 10mm wrench

Step 2: Engine Installation

The engine is the heart of your Rivett. This step requires careful positioning:

  • Lower the engine block onto the motor mounts
  • Connect the transmission to the engine
  • Attach the clutch and flywheel assembly
  • Install the starter motor and alternator

Step 3: Fuel and Electrical Systems

Without fuel and spark, your engine won't run. Complete these systems carefully:

  • Fuel tank: Install in the rear, connect fuel lines
  • Carburetor: Mount on the intake manifold
  • Battery: Place and connect terminals correctly
  • Wiring: Connect ignition, lights, and gauges

Step 4: Body and Interior

Finish your build with body panels and interior components:

  • Install front and rear fenders
  • Mount the doors and adjust alignment
  • Add seats, dashboard, and steering wheel
  • Connect pedals to their respective systems

First Start Checklist

Before turning the key, verify these critical items:

  • Oil level is correct - check the dipstick
  • Coolant is filled in the radiator
  • Fuel tank has gasoline
  • Battery is charged and connected
  • All bolts are tightened
